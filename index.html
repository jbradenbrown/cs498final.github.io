Historical GDP and Recessions in the USA

Since 1947, the USA has tracked quarterly change in GDP to keep tabs on the US economy.

A recession is defined quantitatively as two consecutive quarters of economic contraction. Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is a measure of economic output, and is often what people use to identify recessions. Since GDP scales exponentially, historical comparisons are more easily made using percentage change in GDP. Unfortunately, percentage changes have their own problems. Although it can be ignored when changes are near 0, the magnitudes of sequential changes are not symmetric across 0. A 50% contraction is not sequentially equivalent to a 50% expansion, but a 100% expansion. The contractions in the graph below have been corrected for that distortion.
